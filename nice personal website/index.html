About us

what we aim to do?

Potatoes on mars is an incubator for ‘green startups’. We aim to promote startups that work on innovative and sustainable solutions for slowing down climate change and effectively reducing environmental harm. With us, you can catch up with the latest trends in the ‘green’ startup world, know more about the causes of climate change, how it’s affecting the planet and your role in this. If you have an idea or are working on some initiative we are here to help! The purpose is to provide support and resources to startups, environmental NGOs, school clubs, blogs, and everyone who can and will take action against climate change. Whether you need to launch a marketing campaign or a website, potatoes on mars is with you.

why environmental entrepreneurship

With a population of more that 1.3 billon and growing, India’s natural resources are facing dire shortage. Studies by the World health organisation and Greenpeace have listed nine and seven of the countries cities as the most polluted in the world, respectively to put it mildly, an increased focus on innovative solution is the need of the hour.

can a highly economic activity like entrepreneurship can have healthy environmental effects? ?

Environmental entrepreneurship creates a green industry, which helps to solve environmental problems through the development of innovative solutions. Some companies go green either to reduce environmental harm or to gain consumer’s support. from energy sharing to commodities trading, there’s no limit to the kinds of industries green startups can tackle.

why should entrepreneurs act on climate change now?

Short lived climate pollutants significantly impact air quality, food, water and economic security for much of the world, directly through their negative effects on public health, agriculture and ecosystems. The measures and technologies to reduce short-lived climate pollutants are available today and are practical, technically feasible, and cost-effective. Putting them in place can bring immediate climate benefits, help achieve many global sustainable development goals (SDGs), and improve the health and livelihoods of millions.
